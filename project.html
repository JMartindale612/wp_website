Project

Weaponised Pasts is a cross-disciplinary, Leverhulme-funded project examining the evolution of heritage-based hostility online and in the lab, led by Dr Chiara Bonacchi.

Find out more about the project below.

Overview

Interpretations of the human past are routinely weaponised online to frame others as enemies. Without establishing how such heritage-based hostile narratives spread, and why some may be pervasive on social media, it has not been possible to determine ways of curtailing this phenomenon.

Weaponised Pasts will address this long-standing issue through a novel approach–the first to combine public archaeology, cultural evolution and cognitive psychology.

The team will study the evolution of heritage-based hostility both in ‘real-world’ social media environments and experimentally. Based on our results, we will provide recommendations for limiting the diffusion of heritage-based hostility online.

Objectives

Our investigation will develop a theoretical framework for this weaponisation of the past, and establish its causal mechanisms, by answering the following questions:

  • What leads to the emergence and spread of heritage-based hostility?
  • What role is played by content features, such as analogies used by journalists, the heritage themes, and the general language chosen?
  • What is the impact of the communication context (e.g., prestige bias), and of the values of the social media users that are communicating and receiving this information?
